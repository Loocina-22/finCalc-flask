About Compound Interest

Compound interest calculates the growth of an investment by earning interest on both the initial principal and the accumulated interest over time. It's ideal for understanding how savings or investments grow with regular compounding.

Formula

\[ A = P \left(1 + \frac{r}{n}\right)^{nt} \]

Where:
\( A \): Future value
\( P \): Principal
\( r \): Annual interest rate (decimal)
\( n \): Compounds per year
\( t \): Time (years)
