About Simple Interest

Simple interest calculates interest only on the initial principal, without compounding. It’s commonly used for short-term loans or investments where interest doesn’t accumulate.

Formula

\[ I = P \cdot r \cdot t \] \[ A = P + I \]

Where:
\( I \): Interest earned
\( P \): Principal
\( r \): Annual interest rate (decimal)
\( t \): Time (years)
\( A \): Total amount
